# Die Forward — Zone Bible: THE FROZEN GALLERY

---

## Core Concept / Zone Identity

A place where time stopped all at once.

Not erosion. Not slow decay. One moment — and then nothing moved again. The Frozen Gallery is the underworld's most perfect record: every creature, every pilgrim, every prayer caught mid-breath and held. The cold is absolute. The preservation is flawless.

The horror is not in the death. It's in how alive they look.

- **Element:** Ice / memory
- **Color palette:** Pale blue, crystal white, grey stone, faint warmth of torchlight trapped inside the ice
- **Sounds:** Wind through ice tunnels, the deep groan of settling glaciers, silence so complete you hear blood moving in your own ears
- **Smell:** Nothing. Cold kills smell. The absence is its own wrongness.
- **Temperature:** Lethal. Every breath a cloud. Metal sticks to bare fingers. Moving fast is the only warmth available, and there is nowhere to run.

The Gallery does not pursue you. It does not need to. The cold is patient. It has all the time there is.

---

## Zone Lore

Something in the deepest part of the underworld — below the water, below the dark, below the named things — exhaled once.

That is the theory. Not a consensus, not a certainty. But the evidence: the Gallery exists, and it was frozen in a single instant. Not frozen over time — the way frost creeps — but all at once, the way a held breath ends. Stone walls, open flames, people mid-step: stopped. Held. Kept.

The flames are still here. Torches burning when they were sealed into the ice, the fire somehow alive inside the crystal, casting trapped amber light through pale walls. It makes the Gallery beautiful. Lit from within, like something lantern-lit and almost warm, the light never reaching you.

No one knows what exhaled. The scholars who descended to study the Gallery are here, preserved, mid-note. One of them was smiling when it happened. Whatever they found, they were pleased by it.

The underworld keeps this place because it keeps everything. But the Gallery is different — the underworld didn't create these deaths. It inherited them. And inheritance implies something gave it up.

Whatever exhaled once can exhale again.

## The Mechanic

### CHILL

Cold accumulates. Standing in freezing air, touching ice-encased surfaces, being struck by cold-element attacks — each adds a CHILL stack. CHILL is not pain. It is weight. Numbness. The gradual shutdown of the body's urgency.

- **Each CHILL stack reduces stamina regeneration by 1.**
- **Maximum 3 stacks.** At 3, stamina does not regenerate at all.
- Stacks persist between rooms unless cleared.
- Chill stacks are removed by: the Thermal Flask, fire sources (rare in this zone), the passage of time (1 stack per 2 rooms).

> One stack: your fingers are slow. Two: your legs feel borrowed. Three: you are still moving, but only because you remember that you should be.

**Example:** You take two hits from an Ice Wraith (1 CHILL each). You enter a cache room. Stamina regen is reduced by 2 — you have less recovery time before the next encounter. You find a Thermal Flask. All stacks clear. The warmth is almost painful.

### FREEZE

Some attacks carry the potential to stop a target entirely.

- **FREEZE:** On a successful FREEZE trigger, the target skips its next action entirely. They are locked — mid-lunge, mid-cast, mid-reach — for one turn.
- FREEZE is a probability, not a guarantee. Weapons and abilities that carry FREEZE chance list the percentage.
- **Ice enemies are immune to FREEZE.** They are already cold. Cold does not surprise them.
- Players cannot be frozen — only afflicted with CHILL.

> A frozen enemy does not disappear. It stops. You see the moment of its interruption preserved. There is something familiar about that sight, here.

**Example:** You strike The Preserved with a Frost Shard. The freeze chance triggers. It stops mid-reach, one arm extended. You have one turn of uncontested action. Use it.

### PRESERVED CORPSES

In other zones, corpses are memory — objects of decay, evidence of loss. Here, they are something more disturbing: they are indistinguishable from the living. A frozen body does not gray. Does not bloat. Does not lose the expression it wore at the moment of death.

- **Preserved Corpse discoveries include full physical detail** — what they looked like, what they were doing, what their face was doing. More than other zones. More than you may want.
- **Loot is fully intact.** Nothing rots. Everything works. The weapons are sharp. The food is edible, in a way that makes you think about it too long.
- **Final messages are more detailed.** The cold preserved their words. Their handwriting. The pressure of the stylus. Some messages are very long. They had time.
- The Gallery's preserved dead feel more like people than any corpse you have found before. That is the mechanic. That is the horror.

> They look like they could wake up. They won't. But you will keep checking.

## Bestiary

---

### Tier 1 — Common Horrors

---

#### The Preserved
*Frozen corpses of those who came before. Thawed just enough to move.*

They are not restored. They are not healed. They are simply mobile again — the ice releasing just enough grip that muscles can contract, joints can rotate, a preserved face can turn toward warmth. Toward you.

They remember nothing. They move because cold things thaw toward heat, and you are the only heat.

**Behavior:** REACHING (arms extended, toward warmth), ADVANCING (slow, relentless, no hurry), CROWDING (they follow others — where one goes, more come)

> *Frost on its lashes. Eyes still have color. It was wearing good boots when it died. Someone's name is stitched inside the collar. It is almost certainly someone's name you would recognize, if you stayed long enough to read it.*

---

#### Ice Wraiths
*Spirits that feed on warmth. They leave cold behind like a wake.*

Not creatures of flesh. Not even creatures of form — they are hunger given cold shape, pressing through walls, through air, seeking the heat signatures of the living. When they pass through you, they take something. A degree. A memory of warmth. A fragment of the sensation of a fire.

They cannot be frozen. Cold is where they live.

**Behavior:** PHASING (passing through walls and obstacles), SWARMING (rarely alone), DRAINING (contact applies CHILL stacks, 1 per pass)

> *It has no face. Just the shape of wanting, drifting. Where it was, the air is colder. Where it goes, the air is colder. It is making this room uninhabitable. It is patient about it.*

---

### Tier 2 — Uncommon Threats

---

#### Frost Sentinels
*Ancient guardians sealed in ice armor. They have been standing guard since before the Gallery froze.*

Something placed them here, before the instant of freezing. Something knew this place would need protecting. The armor they wear is not steel — it is ice compressed over centuries until it's harder than stone. It doesn't chip. It deflects. Moving in it should be impossible, and yet.

They are slow. When they strike, they strike once. Once is enough.

**Behavior:** DORMANT (stationary until approached), PURSUING (once triggered, will not stop), GUARDING (positioned at transitions, thresholds, anything worth protecting)

**Immune to FREEZE.**

> *It has not moved in years. The ice on its shoulders has built up in layers, like geological record. You are close enough to see your reflection in its visor. Your reflection turns its head before you do.*

---

#### The Shattered
*Creatures that were frozen and cracked. Each fragment moves on its own.*

When the Gallery froze, some things broke rather than held. The cold found the flaws — stress lines, old wounds, the structural weaknesses of living bodies — and cracked them open. The pieces scattered. The pieces kept moving.

They were one thing. Now each shard is its own incomplete hunger. Killing the largest piece doesn't end it. It creates more.

**Behavior:** SCATTERING (splitting on damage), REFORMING (smaller pieces merge if not separated), SWARMING (many small things, no single target)

> *It moves wrong — not like a body, but like debris. Three pieces coordinating. Four. More as the fight goes. The floor is getting crowded with parts of something that used to be whole.*

---

### Shared Enemies (Cold Variants)

---

#### Flickering Shades (Cold Variant)
Standard shades, slowed and crystalline in this zone. Their blinks are shorter, their phases harder — the cold anchors them partially to space. They are more predictable, but each overlap coats you in a layer of frost.

> *It stutters between positions, trailing cold. Where it lands, frost blooms on the stone.*

---

#### Forgotten Guardians
Stone sentinels, here from before the instant. Some were frozen in motion. Some were frozen standing still. They wake the same as always — grinding, slow, inevitable. The ice on their joints doesn't stop them. It just makes them louder.

> *You thought the creaking was the glacier settling. Then the statue's hand moved.*

---

#### Bone Weavers
Hands from walls, as always — but here the walls are ice. The hands emerge already blue, already numb, already preserved in their reaching. They were here when the cold came. They didn't change.

> *The ice has fingers. They're reaching for you.*

## Boss

### THE GLACIAL SOVEREIGN

*It was the first thing frozen. Everything else is arranged around it.*

The center of the Gallery — the original point. When whatever exhaled, the Sovereign was here. It was here before humans descended into the underworld. Before the Gallery had visitors. Before the underworld had names for places like this.

It was sealed into ice that, over millennia, compressed into something harder than stone. It cannot move quickly. The ice is in its joints, in its lungs, in the space between thoughts. But it moves. Slowly, with the certainty of geological time, it moves.

When it strikes, the air temperature drops twenty degrees in the radius of the blow. Not a spell. Not an effect. The physics of displacing something that cold through that much space — the air cannot hold its heat against it.

It has been waiting. Not for you specifically. For anything warm. You are simply the most recent warmth it has sensed in the years since the last pilgrim came through.

**Behavior Phases:**

**Phase 1 — DORMANT ADVANCE:** Moves toward you with terrible patience. One action per round. Watching. Learning. The temperature drops 1 degree per round simply from its proximity.

**Phase 2 (below 50% HP) — GLACIAL EXHALE:** Releases a breath of frozen air — a wide arc, 2 CHILL stacks on all targets in range. Does not hurry. The exhale is not desperate. It is the natural consequence of breathing.

**Phase 3 (below 25% HP) — THE WEIGHT OF IT:** Begins to move continuously. Still slow, but without pause. Each strike applies FREEZE chance to the room environment — surfaces become slick, obstacles ice over, movement costs increase. The fight is now about managing a room that is becoming actively hostile.

**Immune to FREEZE.** It was the first thing frozen. It remembers.

**On death:**

The ice encasing it cracks in a slow progression, not all at once. One fracture, then another, then a sound like a building settling into its foundation. The cold does not leave the room. The cold was never just the Sovereign. The cold is the zone. The Sovereign was only its oldest expression.

A preserved chamber opens behind it. Whatever was here at the beginning — before the Gallery had a name, before the first pilgrim descended — rests inside. Still perfectly intact.

> *It turns toward you. No urgency. It has spent centuries waiting. A few more minutes means nothing. The temperature in the room has been dropping since you entered. You understand now that you walked into this when you crossed the threshold.*

## Items

### Zone-Specific

---

#### Frost Shard
> A piece of ice that should have melted days ago. It hasn't. It won't.
**Effect:** Melee weapon. Each hit carries a FREEZE chance (15%). On FREEZE trigger, target skips next action. Does not affect ice-immune enemies.

---

#### Thermal Flask
> Metal, dented, containing something that holds heat like a grudge. You can feel it through the casing.
**Effect:** Consumable. Removes all CHILL stacks instantly. One use. The warmth is temporary. The relief is not.

---

#### Cold Pill
> A small white capsule found in someone's pocket. No label. No explanation. You understand what it does without being told.
**Effect:** Consumable. The next hit that would kill you leaves you at 1 HP instead. One use. The cold keeps you here a little longer. Make it worth something.

---

#### Preserved Blade
> Found on a corpse. Still in its sheath. The edge hasn't dulled — cold kills bacteria, kills rust, kills everything that would diminish a weapon. The hand that held it was still gripping when you arrived.
**Effect:** Rare melee weapon. Higher base damage than comparable-tier weapons. Has absorbed something from its previous owner — a warmth it shouldn't have. Heals 1 HP on kill.
> *The blood on the grip is not yours. Not yet.*

## NPCs

---

### The Archivist

A figure that moves between the frozen dead with the focused attention of a scholar at work. It carries a stylus and a book of black pages — the entries white, like frost writing. It catalogs. It records. It knows everyone.

**Dialogue style:** Precise, professional, faintly sorrowful. Speaks of the dead as subjects of study and simultaneously as people it has come to care about. The two impulses do not conflict in it.

> "Entry four-hundred and twelve. Female, late twenties, eastern descent. Died mid-sentence — you can see the breath still in the air above her if you look at the right angle. Her name was Corvel. She was a cartographer's apprentice."

> "You want to know about someone specific? Give me the room number. I know all of them. I've been cataloging this Gallery for — I stopped counting years ago. It seemed counterproductive."

> "They are not gone. Not here. I want you to understand that. They are preserved. When the Gallery ends, whenever that is, all of this will end together. Until then, they are as real as anything."

**Function:** Can identify any frozen corpse by name, profession, how long they've been preserved, and what they were doing at the moment of freezing. Trades in information — knowing the dead gives hints about the zone's history, hidden rooms, and what the Glacial Sovereign was before it froze.

---

### The Thaw

A creature that should not be thawing. Something in the Gallery's deepest section, where the cold is most absolute, has begun to warm — incrementally, over what the Thaw estimates has been fifty years. The Thaw estimates poorly. It loses time.

It is large. It was not human when it froze, though the Gallery has shaped it in the decades of slow melting into something that sometimes stands upright. It is grateful to be moving. It is confused about where it has been. It is not always aware of what it's doing when it reaches out.

**Dialogue style:** Fragmented, earnest, periodically incorrect about basic facts. Long silences between sentences while it locates the thought it was holding. Warm in affect — genuinely grateful, genuinely trying.

> "I was — I was. Before. There was a before."

> "You are warm. You feel — I remember that. Warmth. I used to. I remember — I used to be warm also."

> "Do not stand too close. I am still learning where I end. I think I am still learning where I end."

> "I have been waking for — some time. I do not know what I will be when I am fully awake. I am not certain I was — pleasant. Before."

**Function:** The Thaw has been here since before the instant of freezing. It remembers something of what came before — the Gallery as a living place, whatever the Sovereign was in motion. Its information is fragmented but potentially unique. Interacting with it carries risk — it loses itself occasionally, and its reach is significant.

---

### Echo of the First

Not a creature. A projection — the underworld's attempt to render whoever was frozen first, and its failure to fully reconstruct them. The Echo knows it is incomplete. It is missing the back half of itself, and several decades of memory, and its name.

It has been in the Gallery since it was made. It watched the Preserved arrive, one by one, across years of pilgrimages. It knows the Gallery better than the Archivist, but its knowledge is experienced rather than recorded — it cannot tell you what it knows, only show you where things are.

**Dialogue style:** Calm, clear, and slightly wrong — as if the underworld is assembling it in real time and occasionally substituting the wrong word. Refers to itself in the third person sometimes. Aware that it is a projection, and aware that projections don't survive the things that made them.

> "The first thing frozen. That is what they call it. There is no record of a name. The Echo does not remember having one."

> "The Sovereign was not always sealed. Before the instant, it moved. It was not kind. The Gallery was not — still. There was a before, and the before was not quiet."

> "When you reach the deep room, it will show you something. The Echo cannot tell you what. The Echo has not been to the deep room. The projection does not reach that far."

> "If you find something that belonged to a person, before the instant, and you leave it in the alcove near the far gate — the Echo will still be here when you return. Perhaps more completely."

**Function:** Navigation. The Echo can highlight hidden paths, indicate where Frost Sentinels are dormant versus alert, and gesture toward the Sovereign's chamber when asked. Its hints are spatial — shown, not told. Speaking with it adds lore context about the moment of freezing and what the Gallery was before.

### EXIT

**The Ascending Crack:**
> A fault in the Gallery's ceiling, widened by glacial movement over time, leading upward through ice and stone. Light at the top — real light. > A door in the ice — wooden, impossibly, sealed but not frozen. Warm to the touch. The wood has warped slightly against the cold, the grain expanded. On the other side, the temperature is different. > The Glacial Sovereign's chamber opens through to the other side. The deep room. Beyond it, a passage the Sovereign was blocking — has always been blocking. The passage rises. You can see the gradient of warmth from here, the air lightening as the Gallery's absolute cold gives way to something more manageable. The Sovereign let you through. In a way.

## Audio Direction

### Philosophy

The Gallery sounds like the end of sound. Every noise is precise and isolated — each crack, each footstep, each breath — because there is nothing else. No ambience of living things, no drip of water, no movement of air that wasn't caused by you. The silence is structural. It is what this zone is made of.

Cold kills sound, eventually. What you hear in the Gallery is what the cold hasn't reached yet.

---

### Ambient Layers

**Base layer (constant, subsonic):**
- Deep glacial tone — not quite audible, felt in the chest and back teeth
- Settling ice: low structural groans at long intervals, unpredictable
- The absence of water — specifically, the absence of the drip that defines other zones. It is louder than the drip would be.

**Mid layer (occasional):**
- Wind through ice tunnels — not howling. Breathing. Long, low passages of cold air with no clear source
- Creak of ice under pressure — intermittent, the sound of enormous weight finding new equilibrium
- Distant collapse — deep in the structure, far away, something shifts. Then silence.

**Unsettling layer (rare, triggered):**
- Your own heartbeat, audible. Not amplified — just suddenly audible, because nothing else is louder.
- A sound that was there, that you realize has been there, that stops when you notice it.
- In rooms with The Preserved: breathing. One breath. Then nothing. The Gallery does not confirm whether you imagined it.

---

### Music

**Style:** Dark ambient / drone, colder and sparser than the base zone

- **Instruments:** Bowed cryogenic metal, glass harmonics, processed breath, low string sustain at near-inaudible volumes
- **No rhythm.** Not even the implied rhythm of the water zone's drips. Timeless.
- **Temperature in sound:** The music should feel cold — higher frequencies suppressed, low-end dominant, spaces between notes longer than seems necessary
- **The trapped flames:** Occasional harmonic suggestion of something warm — a flicker, a tone that almost resolves — then returns to cold. The fire is in the ice. It is not available.

| State | Music Feel |
|-------|-----------|
| Exploring | Near-absence. Low sustain beneath hearing. Room tone only. |
| Approaching combat | A single note rises slowly. Doesn't resolve. |
| In combat | Pulse emerges — not heartbeat, but structural. Ice under pressure. |
| Victory | The note that was rising does not resolve. It descends instead. Held. |
| Death | Music ends before you do. The silence is how you know. |
| Finding corpse | A tone that has no source. Present for the reading. Gone after. |
| Near the Sovereign | A harmonic that should not exist — two fundamentally incompatible frequencies held simultaneously. |

---

### Creature Audio

**The Preserved:**
- Movement: Frozen joint creak, the sound of preservation giving way to motion — cartilage working against ice crystal
- Idle: Nothing. Preserved silence.
- Attack: Displacement of frozen air — a sharp exhalation, crystalline
- Death: Re-freezing. The sound of something becoming still again.

**Ice Wraiths:**
- Movement: Temperature differential — a soft hiss as warmer air is displaced by cold passage
- Presence: The room goes quiet before they arrive. Sound fails.
- Attack: Contact is silent. The chill stack appears; you hear your own gasp.
- Dissipation: Nothing. The room is simply colder than it was.

**Frost Sentinels:**
- Movement: Grinding, glacial — ice against stone, compacted armor shifting. Loud. They do not approach quietly.
- Idle: Structural settling. The sound of old weight.
- Attack: The air displacement of something massive. The impact itself. The temperature drop in the aftermath.
- Death: A long, layered cracking — decades of ice compression releasing at once.

**The Shattered:**
- Movement: Scraping, asymmetrical — pieces of different sizes moving at different rhythms across stone
- Multiply: A sharp crack as a piece divides. Then the new piece's first movement.
- Attack: Impact of ice fragment against flesh — dense, cold
- Never silent: There is always scraping. That is how you know where all the pieces are. When the scraping stops from one direction, look there.

**The Glacial Sovereign:**
- Movement: The building groans when it moves. Not its body — the Gallery itself, accommodating the displacement of something ancient
- Breath: Long. Deep. Audible from three rooms away as a sustained low note
- Strike: The temperature drop is audible — a sharp gaseous release, air contracting
- Phase transition: Silence. Then the sound of ice being exhaled.

---

### Room-Specific Audio

**EXPLORE rooms:**
- Base layer only, with long silences between sounds
- Your footsteps on ice-covered stone dominate the soundscape
- The Gallery emphasizes your presence rather than its own

**COMBAT rooms:**
- Enemy sounds replace ambient entirely
- No music baseline — only the pulse building under combat
- Chill stack application: a sound like held breath

**CORPSE rooms:**
- Complete silence during discovery
- The tone that appears has no direction — present in the room rather than from speakers
- Final message reading: no underscoring. Words carry their own weight.

**CACHE rooms:**
- Ambient returns but warmer in character — slight harmonic shift
- If the Archivist is present: the soft scratch of stylus on page, continuous
- Brief suggestion of the trapped flames' warmth in the music layer

**EXIT rooms:**
- Sound of the surface begins to arrive — not a specific sound, but a remembered quality of air
- Music approaches resolution for the first time in the zone
- The cold does not fade. It watches you leave.

## Sample Room Sequence (Frozen Gallery)

1. **EXPLORE** — The Gallery entrance. The transition from other zones is the first warning: the air changes before the ice appears. Your breath becomes visible. The warmth behind you is the last warmth you can reliably locate.

2. **EXPLORE** — First gallery chamber. Forty-seven frozen figures. None of them are looking at you. You check twice.

3. **COMBAT** — Three Preserved, thawed enough to track you. They were pilgrims. You can see what they were carrying when they stopped. They are between you and the next passage.

4. **CORPSE** — A preserved body seated against the wall, face composed. Their pack is frozen open beside them, contents visible. They wrote a long message. They had time.

5. **CACHE** — The Archivist's station. Supplies set aside by hands that know the Gallery's rhythms. The Archivist is present, recording. Entry four-hundred and thirteen. That is you, if you die here.

6. **COMBAT** — A Frost Sentinel at the threshold to the gallery's deeper section. It has been standing here since before the freezing. It woke when you crossed the room's midpoint. It is between you and the Gallery's heart.

7. **EXPLORE** — The deep gallery. The figures here are older. The preservation is older. These are not pilgrims — they predate pilgrimage. Whatever they were, the Gallery keeps them at its core.

8. **COMBAT** — The Glacial Sovereign. It turns toward you. The temperature in the chamber drops as it moves. It has been waiting since before you existed. It does not hurry.

9. **EXIT** — The Sovereign's chamber opens through. The deep room beyond — the Archivist never came this far; this is not in the catalog. The passage rises. The cold remains, but it releases you. It knows what you've done. It lets you carry it out with you.

## Content Generation Guidelines

When generating room descriptions, combat narration, or death text for the Frozen Gallery:

1. **Stay in second person, present tense**
2. **Lead with temperature and silence before sight** — cold is sensory before visual
3. **Keep room descriptions under 50 words; let the preserved dead do the weight**
4. **Vary sentence length — frozen zone warrants longer silences between sentences, shorter punches for action**
5. **Never explain what froze the Gallery** — imply, describe effects, never name a cause
6. **The preserved dead are specific individuals** — give them boots, give them names stitched into collars, give them the expression they wore at the moment. Specificity is the horror.
7. **Cold is patient, not aggressive** — threats advance slowly. The dread is in the certainty, not the speed.
8. **CHILL and FREEZE are physical, not magical** — describe cold accumulating in the body. Stamina failure is the body forgetting urgency. Freeze is a thing stopping, not a spell being cast.
9. **Preserved Corpse messages earn their length** — these are more detailed than other zones. Use the space. They had time to write. Let it show.
10. **The Sovereign is geological time given attention** — it does not threaten. It turns toward you the way a glacier turns. The dread is in the scale, not the malice.
11. **Never use: suddenly, very, really, exclamation marks** — the Gallery has no use for urgency in its narration. If it is urgent, make it urgent through compression and specificity, not emphasis.
12. **The cold outlasts you** — every exit text, every near-escape, every moment of warmth: end it with the knowledge that the cold is still there. You left it. You carry a piece of it out.
