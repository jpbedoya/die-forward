# Die Forward — Zone Bible: THE LIVING TOMB

---

## Core Concept / Zone Identity

**Element:** Organic / flesh

**Tone:** Something grew in the dark while no one was watching. Now everything is part of it. The walls breathe. The floor is warm. You are a foreign body. The tomb is not attacking you. It is rejecting you — methodically, intimately, without malice.

The horror here is not death. The horror is warmth. The horror is that it feels, in some cellular way, like being held.

---

### Sensory Profile

**Color palette:** Wet red where things are alive. Sickly green where they are becoming something else. Grey-pink membrane where flesh has sealed over stone. Pale yellow in the places where something died inside the walls long ago and was absorbed anyway.

**Sounds:** A heartbeat. Not yours. In the walls, in the floor, in the ceiling — constant, measured, patient. Wet sounds beneath that. Digestion. Breathing. The occasional low moan from something that is not a throat but functions as one.

**Smell:** Iron. Yeast. Something warm and animal. The inside of a living thing — close, organic, wrong. It is not rot. Rot is what happens when things die. This is the smell of something very much alive.

**Temperature:** Body temperature. Exactly. The air, the walls, the floor — all of it. 98.6 degrees of wrongness. It does not feel like warmth. It feels like being swallowed.

---

## Zone Lore

The underworld is not all stone and water. In one section — no one agrees on which one, or when it started — something began growing.

The oldest records, scratched in passages no one enters anymore, suggest it started small. A patch of growth on a chamber wall. Soft. Moist. Organic in a place that should be mineral. The few who noticed assumed it was mold, fungus, something mundane. They did not come back to check.

No one knows what it is. No one who has spent long enough to know has been able to leave and explain it. Theories persist among those who study such things:

The underworld's immune response. Too many intruders. Too many foreign bodies walking its passages, leaving their stories, their blood, their corpses. What grows in the Living Tomb might be the underworld's attempt to process them — to absorb and incorporate rather than simply collect. The dead become part of the architecture elsewhere. Here, they become part of something that breathes.

Something else entirely. A seed that fell in from somewhere the underworld connects to. A passenger from one of the things that shouldn't have come through. Whatever it was, it found the conditions acceptable. It is comfortable here. It is thriving.

It does not appear to want anything in particular. It accepts everything. It incorporates everything. It has no agenda beyond continuation. This is, in its own way, the most disturbing possibility.

The Living Tomb is patient in the way that organisms are patient — not planning, not malicious, simply persistent. It was here before you arrived. It will be here after. If you die inside it, you become part of it.

Some who entered looking for the boundary of the growth have found, turning around to retrace their steps, that the passage behind them is subtly changed. Softer. Warmer. That the stone they were certain they'd walked over is now something that gives slightly underfoot.

The Tomb is growing. It has always been growing.

---

## The Mechanic: INFECTION

### What It Is

Some enemies in the Living Tomb carry the Tomb's growth. On contact — a claw, a spore, a brush against a wall that has become something it shouldn't be — they transmit it. This is not venom. It is not poison. It is incorporation. The Tomb is beginning the process of making you part of itself.

**INFECTION stacks accumulate. They do not decay on their own.**

---

### Stack Thresholds

**3 stacks — Consumption:**
One item in your inventory is dissolved. The Tomb recognizes useful material when it finds it. The consumed item is gone. There is a brief sensation of warmth where the item was — pouch, pocket, belt. Then nothing.

Which item is consumed is random. Rare items are not protected. Plan accordingly.

**5 stacks — Suppression:**
-30% damage dealt. The Tomb is not killing you — it is quieting you. Making you slower. Gentler. Easier to incorporate. Your strikes feel like they're landing in something soft.

**Beyond 5 stacks:**
The system continues. Nothing new is announced. But you are running out of time.

---

### How Infection Spreads

**Primary sources:** Mycelium Crawlers (spore injection on hit). The Bloom (proximity infection — standing near it is enough).

**Environmental sources:** Certain walls in EXPLORE rooms. Certain floors in passages that have fully converted. The game will describe these: *The wall gives slightly when you touch it.* Do not touch it.

**Secondary spread:** Being at 3+ stacks increases infection stack gain from all sources. The Tomb recognizes what is already being incorporated.

---

### Cure Items

**Rare. Prioritize them above almost everything else.**

Single-stack removal exists but requires resources. Full cleanse items are legendary drops. The Surgeon NPC can cure — at a cost.

---

### Strategic Implications

The mechanic punishes patience. The standard underworld strategy — conserve resources, fight carefully, pick your moments — fails here. Long fights mean more hits mean more stacks. Kill quickly or be consumed.

Conversely: fleeing is legitimate. FLEE actions are not shameful here. They are survival.

**Build around infection:**
- Items that reduce the number of hits you take
- Items that accelerate killing speed
- Purge Salt as the highest-priority pickup of the zone
- The Immune Draught for boss fights

**Recognize when you're losing:**
At 4 stacks, assess honestly. Do you have a cure? Is the fight winnable in 2-3 more turns? If not, flee. Reaching 5 stacks and surviving long enough to cure is possible but requires resources you likely need later.

## Bestiary

---

### Tier 1 — Common Horrors

---

#### Mycelium Crawlers
*New enemy.*

Something between fungal fruiting body and insect. Too many legs — the number seems to vary depending on when you look. They move in short, fast bursts punctuated by stillness. Their caps — if those are caps — pulse when they're about to strike.

They inject spores on contact. This is the primary infection vector in the zone. Every hit from a Crawler adds one infection stack.

They are individually fragile. They are frequently not individual.

**Behavior:** SKITTERING (erratic burst movement), INJECTING (striking and withdrawing), CLUSTERING (they move toward each other when threatened)

> *The floor clicks. Eight legs. Twelve. You stop counting.*

---

#### The Incorporated
*New enemy.*

They were people once. Players, creatures, things that wandered in. The Tomb has absorbed and partially extruded them — shapes pushed back out of the membrane not because the Tomb released them, but because it needed them to move.

They are not dead. Their eyes still register you. Some of them try to speak. What comes out is not language.

They do not attack because they want to. They attack because the Tomb wants them to. The distinction matters, but it doesn't help.

**Behavior:** REACHING (arms extended, palms up — supplication or aggression, it's unclear), DRAGGING (slow, encumbered, trailing connections back into the wall), TRANSMITTING (infection on touch — what the Tomb already has, it shares)

> *It looks at you. Something behind its eyes is still trying to say something. The rest of it keeps moving forward.*

---

#### The Unfinished
*Shared enemy (zone variant).*

In the Living Tomb, The Unfinished are farther along than elsewhere. They've begun incorporating Tomb tissue. The asymmetry isn't just their original condition anymore — one arm has been replaced by something pink and membrane-wrapped. An eye socket hosts something that blinks at a different rate.

They don't seem to notice. Or they've stopped noticing.

**Behavior:** LURCHING (as elsewhere), ADAPTING (faster here — the Tomb helps them adapt), INCORPORATING (they absorb pieces of the environment as they move, briefly growing larger)

> *Half of it is still what it used to be. The other half is becoming something that doesn't have a name yet.*

---

#### Bloated Ones
*Shared enemy (zone variant).*

In the Living Tomb, the Bloated Ones have been incorporated rather than simply drowned. What they leak is not dark water — it is something warm. What they release on rupture is not memory alone. There are spores in the cloud. Infection on burst.

Stay back when killing them. Strike from range if you have it.

**Behavior:** SHAMBLING (slow, straining), RUPTURING (explosive death — back away), SEEDING (the burst cloud lingers and infects — don't stand in it)

> *It moves toward you with terrible patience. Its skin is taut. Something inside it is pressurized.*

---

#### The Congregation (Absorbed Variant)
*Shared enemy (zone variant).*

The Congregation in the Living Tomb has been incorporated. They still move as one — still press together, still chant — but they have fused more completely. There are no gaps between them. They are one organism with many mouths, all speaking in unison.

What they chant has changed. It is not a prayer anymore. It sounds more like a digestive rhythm.

**Behavior:** PROCESSING (they move as a mass — slowly but relentlessly), EMBRACING (pulling you into the mass — infection on contact), PULSING (they breathe in unison, the chant synchronized with heartbeat)

> *They move together. Breathe together. They have been together long enough that the seams between them are gone.*

---

### Tier 2 — Uncommon Threats

---

#### Membrane Guardians
*New enemy.*

Massive. They block passages — not by standing in them, but by *being* the passage at that point. Sphincter-like, pulsing, they have no discernible front or back. When they seal, the room seals. When they open, you may pass — at a cost.

They can be fought. This takes time and multiple strikes. During the fight, they are capable of infection on contact. They seal the room when engaged, preventing escape unless they're already defeated or badly damaged.

**Behavior:** SEALING (room lockdown on combat initiation), CONTRACTING (periodic defensive closure — reduced damage taken), LEAKING (drips infecting fluid when damaged), RELAXING (open when heavily damaged — possible flee window)

> *The passage ahead is breathing. Slowly, rhythmically. It is aware of you. It is deciding.*

---

#### The Bloom
*New enemy.*

It is a walking infection event. Where it touches, the Tomb expands — floor, wall, creature, you. It is not aggressive in any recognizable sense. It drifts. It meanders. It leaves conversion behind it like a wake.

Proximity is enough. You do not need to be struck by The Bloom to be infected by it. Standing within arm's reach of it accumulates stacks. The infected ground it leaves behind also infects for a short time.

Killing it stops the spread. The converted ground it's left behind does not unconvert.

**Behavior:** DRIFTING (slow, no direct pathing toward you — just moving), RADIATING (constant proximity infection), CONVERTING (any organic surface it touches joins the Tomb), SPLITTING (at half health, briefly divides — two smaller Blooms, each still infecting)

> *It doesn't come for you. It doesn't need to. It just needs to be in the same room.*

## Boss: THE ROOT

### Description

The Root is not a creature that occupies the final chamber. The Root is the final chamber.

Every wall is it. Every surface of floor, ceiling, every passage wall. The flesh of the Tomb reaches its fullest expression here — wet, red, pulsing, alive in a way that makes the rest of the zone feel like a sketch. There is no separate entity to fight. There is only the room.

You will spend a moment looking for the thing you're supposed to fight. There is no thing. The chamber itself addresses this, in its way: the floor rises. The ceiling descends. A wall reaches.

### Narrative Context

The Root may be the origin. Or the center. Or the oldest part. It has been growing for long enough that it no longer resembles what it might have started as. Other zones have bosses — creatures, entities, specific things with intentions. The Root has the intention of the Tomb: incorporation. It is doing what it has always done. You have simply come close enough to matter.

### Behaviors

**PERISTALSIS:** The floor contracts in waves. A squeeze of pressure moving through the chamber. Dodge or be ground against the rising ceiling.

**EXTRUSION:** Limb-like shapes push out of the walls — not claws, not arms, but shapes in that direction. They seek. They grab. They pull toward the membrane if they connect. Infection on contact.

**SEALING:** Passages back close. Not permanently — they cycle, open and close. There is always a way through. But not always now.

**PULSE:** A full chamber pressure wave. The Root contracts completely for a moment — everything closes in. The chamber is briefly, terrifyingly small. Then it releases. During the release: the one moment its surfaces are pulling back rather than extending, offering a window for strikes.

**ABSORPTION:** If you stand still long enough in one place, the floor begins to incorporate your feet. Warmth first. Then weight. Keep moving.

**SUMMONING:** The Root extruded things into the Tomb throughout. It can extrude them here too. Mycelium Crawlers from the walls. The Incorporated from the floor. Mid-fight additions that split your attention.

### Special Rules

- There is no visible health bar. The Root's damage state is described through the room itself — the walls thin, the pulse slows, the color shifts from wet red toward pale.
- Infection still accrues here. The chamber itself infects on sustained contact with surfaces.
- You cannot flee from the Root. The exits are sealed until the Root is sufficiently damaged, at which point they open (it opens them — a choice, or simply a consequence of its damage state).

### Why It Exists

The Tomb has been growing toward something. The Root is what it grows toward — a center of mass, a locus of incorporation. It is not the Tomb's will. It is the Tomb's body. Fighting it is fighting a place. Surviving it is surviving the idea that a place can want to keep you.

> *The chamber breathes. Every surface. Every inch. It has been waiting — not for you specifically. For something to digest. You are the something it found.*

## Items

### Consumables

#### Purge Salt
> Coarse white crystals, blood-warm to the touch. What they purge is not toxin — it is *incorporation*. The body does not enjoy the process.
**Effect:** Remove all current infection stacks. Immediate. Single use.
**Rarity:** Uncommon (zone-specific)

---

#### Sealed Wrapping
> Treated cloth that resists penetration. The treatment is not pleasant to consider. It works.
**Effect:** Prevents the next infection stack gained. One charge. Consumed on use.
**Rarity:** Common (zone-specific)

---

#### Immune Draught
> Bitter, grey, tastes like lye. Drink it and the Tomb cannot find you — briefly. You feel less warm. That is the goal.
**Effect:** Temporary infection immunity — cannot gain infection stacks for a short duration. Recommended before The Root.
**Rarity:** Rare (zone-specific)

---

### Weapons

#### Parasite Blade
> The edge is threaded with something that moves when you're not watching it. It knows what the Tomb's cells taste like. It is pleased to find them.
**Type:** Melee. Heals on hit versus infected enemies or enemies with infection stacks. The healing is small. It adds up.
**Rarity:** Uncommon (zone-specific)

---

### Artifacts (Passive Effects)

#### Cauterized Skin
> A scar that crosses your palm — not yours, or not originally. Where infection tries to enter, it finds this instead.
**Effect:** Reduce infection stacks gained from all sources by 1 (minimum 0). Does not prevent The Bloom's aura. Prevents most single-contact infections.
**Rarity:** Rare

---

#### Borrowed Heartbeat
> A small piece of something — organic, pulsing faintly. It syncs with the Tomb rather than fighting it. This makes you harder to notice.
**Effect:** Reduce aggression of Incorporated and passive Bloom drift. You are, to a minimal and temporary degree, indistinguishable from furniture. Do not press this advantage.
**Rarity:** Uncommon

---

#### Surgeon's Thread
> Black, impossibly fine. It closes wounds before they can be incorporated. The thread knows what isn't supposed to grow there.
**Effect:** Infection stacks above 2 decay by 1 per combat room cleared, rather than persisting indefinitely.
**Rarity:** Rare

## NPCs

---

### The Symbiont

It sits in an alcove that is also, partially, part of it. The wall has grown into one shoulder. The floor has risen around one leg. This has been happening for long enough that it does not appear to cause distress.

It is helpful. Earnestly, completely helpful. It has made peace with its situation and wants to share what it knows about the zone, because it has had a great deal of time to learn.

Its voice comes from a place that is no longer entirely a throat. The sound is wet. The information is accurate.

**Dialogue style:** Calm, informative, unsettling in its equanimity. Does not acknowledge the wall growing through its shoulder as a problem. References the Tomb warmly.

**Function:** Zone information. Warns about upcoming encounters. Can identify items for a small cost. Has opinions about The Root that are not entirely its own.

> *"The Bloom is slower than it looks. You have more room than you think. Stay along the far wall — the incorporated section there hasn't fully woken."*
>
> *It shifts in the alcove. The wall shifts with it.*
>
> *"I've been here a long time. It's not what you'd expect. Warmer."*

---

### The Surgeon

A figure in something that was white once. The instruments are clean — obsessively, almost aggressively clean, which is striking given the environment. They will cure your infection. They will not enjoy it. You will enjoy it less.

Infection curing costs health. The Surgeon cuts out what is being incorporated. This is not metaphorical.

**Dialogue style:** Clinical, direct, quietly horrified by the Tomb (unlike the Symbiont). Every word clipped. Efficiency over comfort. They are keeping themselves together through the discipline of their work.

**Function:** Removes all infection stacks in exchange for HP (scaled to stacks removed). Can also examine your current infection level and advise. Will not stay in one place — encountered randomly in CACHE rooms.

> *"Sit still. This will take a moment."*
>
> *The instruments are cold. Nothing else in here is cold. You appreciate it.*
>
> *"Three stacks. You let it get to three. The third one has already started. This is going to hurt."*

---

### Voice from the Wall

No body is visible. The membrane in this particular passage is thicker than elsewhere, and the voice comes from somewhere behind or inside it. The words are slow — each one considered, as if the process of speaking through this medium requires effort.

It was absorbed long ago. It has had time. It knows things.

**Dialogue style:** Slow, considered, ancient. Speaks in observations rather than answers. Has lost track of time and does not consider this a loss. Occasionally says something so accurate about your current situation that it becomes difficult to dismiss.

**Function:** Lore delivery. Hints about the Root's behaviors. Can reveal the location of Purge Salt caches. Can describe what happened to specific previous players whose corpses are in the zone.

> *"You smell like the surface. They all do, at first."*
>
> *A pause. Something shifts behind the membrane.*
>
> *"The Root is not angry. It is not anything. You are standing in a stomach, traveler. It does not dislike you."*
>
> *Another pause. Longer.*
>
> *"I don't dislike you either. This is, genuinely, not the worst way to spend eternity. I have some suggestions, when you are ready."*

## Room Description Templates

---

### EXPLORE Rooms

**Passage / Organic Corridor:**
> The walls are wrong. Still roughly stone — the shape of a passage, the suggestion of carved work — but warm to the touch, and soft where they should be hard. Something pulses through the material at long, measured intervals. The floor ahead glistens. Move carefully. Move quickly.

---

**Chamber / Enclosed Space:**
> A chamber — large enough that the ceiling is invisible above the growth. The walls are fully converted here, red-grey and slow-breathing. Stalactites of organic material hang from above, gently dripping. The room does not feel empty. It feels occupied.

---

**Threshold / Deeper Entry:**
> Something has sealed the doorway and unsealed it again — the frame is outlined in membrane, recently torn. Beyond it, the air is warmer. The sound of the heartbeat is louder. You are going deeper into it. There is no version of going deeper into it that is safer.

---

**Flooded Passage (Organic):**
> Fluid to your shins. It is not water. It is warm, slightly viscous, and does not smell like water. Something moves beneath the surface with slow, digestion-like purpose. Your feet find the floor. Keep moving.

---

**Narrow Passage / Peristaltic:**
> The passage constricts. Slowly, rhythmically. Wide enough to pass, then narrower, then wide again. The timing is long — three seconds open, one second closed. Wait. Read the rhythm. Move between contractions.

---

**Converted Chamber / Witness:**
> Everything in here has been incorporated. The shapes of furniture, of bones, of items — all sealed beneath a layer of grey-pink membrane. Something that might be a face presses against the inner surface of the far wall, mouth open, frozen in the moment of absorption. It is still. It has been still for a long time.

---

**Junction / Choice:**
> Two ways. Left is darker — the walls haven't converted yet, stone still stone, cold. Right is warm, lit by the bioluminescence of the growth, the heartbeat louder. One way is worse. You are not certain which.

---

**Old Growth / Deep Tomb:**
> This section has been part of the Tomb for a long time. The conversion is total — no stone visible, no original architecture. The walls are smooth, wet, biological. The air here smells of iron and something almost floral. Deep growth. Old growth. Whatever is in here has had time to become entirely what it wants to be.

---

### COMBAT Rooms

**Ambush / Crawlers:**
> The floor clicks. Once. Twice. The pattern breaks apart — not two, not four. Many. They pour from gaps in the membrane and skitter toward you with terrible speed. Each one individually small. Together they are a problem. Kill them fast. Don't let them stack.

---

**Confrontation / Incorporated:**
> It stands in the center of the chamber. Still. Its head tilts toward you with slow recognition. Something behind its eyes is still trying — trying to say something, trying to reach you across whatever the Tomb has done to the space between you. Then it moves. Whatever it was trying to say will have to wait.

---

**The Bloom Encounter:**
> You smell it first — the Tomb smell, but concentrated. Then warmth radiating from the far end of the chamber. Then you see it: slow, drifting, trailing conversion behind it across the floor. It doesn't appear to know you're here. It will infect you from across the room regardless. Do not let this become a long fight.

---

**Guardian / Sealed Room:**
> The passage breathes. Slowly, rhythmically, like a sleeping animal. You step close and it knows you — the rhythm changes. It contracts. The chamber behind you contracts with it. The room has sealed. This will not be resolved by going back.

---

**Swarm / Incorporated and Crawlers:**
> They come from the walls — literally from the walls, extruded, pushed out by something that wants the room defended. The Incorporated first, slow and reaching. The Crawlers behind them, fast and clicking. The Tomb is aware of you and has sent what it has.

---

**Feeding / Distracted Bloom:**
> The Bloom drifts across a chamber, moving over a corpse that has been there long enough to be partially incorporated. It is converting what remains. It hasn't noticed you. You can see a route around the far wall — narrow, risky. Or you can kill it now, while it's occupied, before it finishes and turns.

---

### CORPSE Rooms

**Recent Infection Death:**
> Face down. The body is warm — warmer than the room, which is wrong. The floor has begun the process of incorporating them. If you linger, you can see it progressing. The words scratched into the nearby stone are still fresh: [FINAL MESSAGE].

---

**Old Incorporated Corpse:**
> Only partially visible. The membrane has grown over everything below the shoulders. The face is still clear — expression uncertain, not fearful. They may have gone slowly. The scratched message beside the preserved face reads: [FINAL MESSAGE].

---

**Surrounded Death:**
> They died surrounded by Crawlers — the bodies are here too, killed before the end, but not before they'd done enough. Four stacks, maybe five. You count the sealed wrappings scattered around them. They came prepared. It wasn't enough. Their words, scratched while they could still hold the tool: [FINAL MESSAGE].

---

**Peaceful Absorbed Death:**
> Seated against the wall. Hands open, resting at their sides. The wall has grown into their back, and they did not move against it. The expression is not peaceful exactly — but it is finished. Their message is long, careful, written while they still had time: [FINAL MESSAGE].

---

**Heroic / Fighting Death:**
> The Bloom they killed is here. Already decomposing back into the floor. They killed it and then the infection finished what the Bloom started. A trade they made deliberately — you can see it in how the stacks must have gone. The message is brief, satisfied: [FINAL MESSAGE].

---

### CACHE Rooms

**Sealed Alcove:**
> A room that has not converted. Stone — actual stone, cold to the touch, blessedly cold. The Tomb stops at the threshold here, as if something about this chamber resists it. Supplies sit in the dry corners, untouched. Take what you need. Don't ask why this place is safe. It may not last.

---

**The Surgeon's Station:**
> Clean instruments. Cold — he keeps them cold somehow. The Surgeon is here. Behind them, supplies, neatly organized. You can purchase treatment or resupply, in that order. The smell of antiseptic cuts through the Tomb's warmth like a mercy.

---

**Dry Junction:**
> Where two passages meet, the growth has not followed. A seam in the Tomb's coverage, temporary or structural. Items have accumulated here — others left them, or they were here before the Tomb reached this far. Brief. Use it. The heartbeat is quiet enough here to think.

---

**Bioluminescent Pool:**
> A pool of something clear, faintly glowing. Not the Tomb's fluid — this is older, separate, chemical. Cool to the touch. It would not be advisable to drink it, but beside it, the Tomb's growth is sparse, and the warmth is slightly less. Rest. Resupply. The light is almost comforting.

---

### EXIT Rooms

**The Seam:**
> The Tomb ends. Precisely, as if cut — warm on one side, cold on the other. The passage ahead is stone again. Actual stone, with the cold of deep earth rather than the warmth of something living. The heartbeat fades behind you. Your own heartbeat sounds wrong by comparison — too fast, too loud. You made it out. This part of it.

---

**The Extrusion:**
> The Tomb has grown around the exit without quite closing it — a passage framed in membrane, open, lit by whatever is beyond. It does not look like an exit. It looks like a mouth. Technically it is not a mouth. The passage is clear. Walk through it. Don't think about what it looks like.

---

**The Release:**
> You feel it — a loosening. The floor stops gripping. The warmth reduces by degrees, step by step, as the passage climbs. The heartbeat falls below hearing. By the time you see light ahead, you have been separate from the Tomb for long enough that your own temperature feels cold again. Good. Cold is correct. Cold means outside.

## Audio Direction

---

### Philosophy

The Living Tomb is alive. The audio reflects biology, not geology. Every sound has an organic source. Silence is briefly possible, but the ambient heartbeat never fully stops — it is the zone's base layer, its breathing, its claim on you.

Nothing mechanical. Nothing built. Only the sounds of things that grow.

---

### Ambient Layers

**Base layer (constant):**
- Heartbeat. Slow — approximately 55 BPM. Irregular enough to feel organic rather than metronomic. Present in all rooms. Subtly louder as you descend.
- Room breathing — a low, slow oscillation of the room tone, synced loosely to the heartbeat. The space itself inhales and exhales.
- Distant wet sounds — peristalsis, digestion, nothing you can identify exactly.

**Mid layer (occasional):**
- Dripping of warm fluid — distinct from the Threshold zone's cold water drips. Thicker sound. Slower.
- The Incorporated, distant — faint attempts at sound, not words.
- Growth sounds — very low, almost subsonic. The material of the walls extending.

**Unsettling layer (rare, triggered):**
- Something swallowing. Large. Far away.
- Your heartbeat syncing with the room's heartbeat — a moment where they align. Disturbing because it feels comfortable.
- A sound like the room recognizing you. You cannot describe it afterward.

---

### Music

**Style:** Biological ambient / drone with organic texture

**Instruments:** Low sustained strings, processed breath recordings, waterphone played slowly, deep vocal drones, organic percussion (heartbeat as rhythm element — not aggressive, just present)

**Texture:** Warm. This is important. The music of the Living Tomb should have warmth — not comfort, but warmth. The wrongness is that the warmth is present in the horror. The score should never feel cold. Cold would be a relief.

**No melody.** Texture only. The heartbeat is the only rhythmic element.

| State | Music Feel |
|-------|------------|
| Exploring | Slow organic drone, heartbeat low in mix, breathing texture |
| Approaching combat | Drone thickens, heartbeat slightly louder, wet sounds increase |
| In combat | Heartbeat prominent, rhythm implied rather than stated, pressure |
| Victory | Drone recedes, heartbeat slows, silence that feels like digestion finishing |
| Death | Music stops. Heartbeat continues. Then the heartbeat becomes everything. Then it incorporates you. |
| Finding corpse | Drone sustains a single warm, low note. It holds too long. |

---

### Creature Audio

**Mycelium Crawlers:**
- Movement: Wet clicking, irregular leg-strike patterns, rapid
- Idle: Low spore-release sound, barely audible — like breathing but from a plant
- Attack: Click burst, wet impact, brief silence after injection
- Death: Rapid clicking that stops abruptly. One soft sound. Fluid.

**The Incorporated:**
- Movement: Wet dragging, the sound of something not fully detached
- Idle: Attempted speech — not words, but the shape of words. Tragic.
- Attack: A reaching sound, effort, warmth
- Death: A sound of relief, if relief had the wrong quality

**Membrane Guardians:**
- Movement: They don't move — you hear them breathe
- Idle: Slow rhythmic contraction, like a heartbeat through water
- Attack: Rapid contraction, pressure sound, sealing
- Death: Long slow release of tension, a sound like puncture, then silence

**The Bloom:**
- Movement: No footsteps — it drifts. A low consistent warm tone follows it.
- Idle: The conversion sound of surfaces it contacts — a spreading warmth-sound
- Death: The tone stops. The warmth recedes from its location.

---

### Room-Specific Audio

**EXPLORE rooms:** Full ambient layers. Heartbeat at conversational volume. Organic sounds in mid layer. Exploration should feel like being inside something.

**COMBAT rooms:** Heartbeat louder — urgency. The room is responding to conflict. Creature sounds forward in mix. Ambient doesn't disappear — the Tomb is paying attention.

**CORPSE rooms:** Heartbeat quiets — not absent, but respectful. A single warm, low drone for the reading. The Tomb is not indifferent to its dead. They are part of it. It mourns in its way.

**CACHE rooms:** Heartbeat reduced (in unconverted spaces: nearly absent). Brief relief in the mix. The audio temperature drops slightly.

**EXIT rooms:** Heartbeat fades step by step as you ascend. By the exit, only an echo of it remains. Then even that goes. The silence afterward is enormous.

## Sample Room Sequence

A nine-room demonstration of the Living Tomb's rhythm.

---

**Room 1 — EXPLORE:**
The warmth is immediate. You felt it the moment the passage changed — the stone here does not feel like stone. The floor gives slightly. The walls breathe. Somewhere behind the surface of everything, a heartbeat. Slow. Patient. Not yours.

---

**Room 2 — EXPLORE:**
A narrow passage. Walls close enough to touch on both sides. You do not touch them. Ahead, the bioluminescence begins — pale red and green, pulsing with the heartbeat. The way your torchlight interacts with it is wrong in a way you cannot immediately identify.

---

**Room 3 — COMBAT:**
The Crawlers come from a gap in the converted wall. Twelve of them, maybe more. Fast. Each hit adds a stack. You have two stacks before you've killed half of them. Kill the rest faster.

---

**Room 4 — EXPLORE:**
A chamber with an Incorporated standing in the center, still. It tracks your movement with its head. It makes a sound. You move past it carefully, giving it distance. It follows the sound of your feet until you leave the room. You hear it behind you for longer than seems possible.

---

**Room 5 — CORPSE:**
The body is warm. The floor has grown into the legs. One hand is sealed. The other was free long enough to write: [FINAL MESSAGE]. You note their infection stacks from the description of what happened. They reached five. You have two. Stay aware.

---

**Room 6 — CACHE:**
Stone. Cold, real stone. The heartbeat is quiet here. The Surgeon is in the corner with their instruments, watching you assess your stacks. Two stacks, they say. Manageable. They'll watch your progress. There are supplies here. Take the Sealed Wrapping.

---

**Room 7 — COMBAT:**
The Bloom. Slow, drifting, warm. The infection starts before you engage — one stack from proximity. You close fast and strike hard. Three rounds. Two stacks gained. You leave the room with four stacks total. You have one Sealed Wrapping left.

---

**Room 8 — EXPLORE:**
The deep growth. Stone entirely invisible. The walls move — not dramatically, not in attack, just the slow breathing of something very large and very old. The heartbeat is loudest here. It is possible, for a moment, to feel it in your chest alongside your own. You move faster.

---

**Room 9 — COMBAT:**
The passage is sealed. A Membrane Guardian. It knows you're here. The room contracts. The fight takes three minutes of careful strikes and careful dodging. You come out of it at four stacks, one item consumed. The Purge Salt you were saving: now. Use it now.

## Content Generation Guidelines

Zone-specific rules for all generated content in the Living Tomb.

---

1. **Temperature is always present.** Every room description should contain at least one temperature reference. The warmth is the zone's signature. It should accumulate — the reader should feel progressively more enclosed in it.

2. **Biology, not geology.** Replace stone/mineral language with organic equivalents where the zone has converted: membrane, tissue, pulse, breath, warmth. Use geological language for unconverted areas — the contrast matters.

3. **The horror is intimacy.** Never describe the Tomb as hostile, aggressive, or threatening. It is accepting. It is warm. It wants you close. The dread comes from the wrongness of that warmth, not from violence.

4. **Infection is not punishment — it is incorporation.** When describing infection stacks accumulating, frame it as the Tomb recognizing you, not attacking you. The language should be clinical and biological, not dramatic.

5. **The heartbeat is never absent.** In room descriptions, the heartbeat should be referenced (heard, felt through the floor, sensed) in at least every other room. It is the Tomb's breathing. You are inside something breathing.

6. **Sensitivity for The Incorporated.** They were people. This should be honored. Their behavior should be described with compassion alongside threat — they are not monsters. They are occupants.

7. **Short sentences in combat rooms.** The Tomb is patient. Combat is not. Sentence length should shorten in combat descriptions — the contrast with the zone's general slow rhythm creates urgency.

8. **Smells first in new areas.** The Living Tomb announces itself through smell. Lead EXPLORE room descriptions with olfactory detail before visual. Iron. Yeast. Warmth. The inside of a living thing.

9. **The exit is the zone releasing you, not you escaping.** Frame exits as the Tomb's permission, not your victory. It let you leave. It will let you come back. It is not defeated.

10. **Avoid: gore, clinical medical language, disgust framing.** The Tomb should be unsettling, not repulsive. Intimacy as threat, not visceral horror. The reader should feel enclosed, not nauseated.

---

*When in doubt: ask what the Tomb would say. It would say: stay. It would say: you're already warm. It would say: this is easy. That's the horror.*
