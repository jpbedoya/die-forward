# Die Forward — Zone Bible: THE ASHEN CRYPTS

---

## Core Concept / Zone Identity

The Ashen Crypts reward aggression. Hesitation is a choice. Hesitation kills you.

Where the Threshold is patient — cold water, slow drowning — the Crypts are urgent. The fire doesn't wait. It never waited. This is a place that ended itself rather than surrender, and the ending never finished. The war is over. The burning is not.

You move through ash and heat and the bones of a civilization that chose its own destruction. The enemy here doesn't lurk. It charges. It burns. It spreads.

So do you. Or you don't survive.

- **Element:** Fire / ash
- **Color palette:** Deep orange, charcoal black, bone-white ash, dull ember red
- **Sounds:** Distant crackle, falling ash, wind through burnt stone, the soft pop of cooling embers
- **Smell:** Char, sulfur, and something sweet that has no business being here
- **Temperature:** Dry heat. Suffocating. The air tastes burnt.

---

## Zone Lore

This was a city once.

It had a name. The name burned with everything else. What remains is structure — walls that held their shape through centuries of heat, passages carved by hands that knew what was coming and built anyway. A people who looked at what climbed from below and made a choice: we burn this. We burn all of it. We do not let it have us.

The fire worked. The things from below did not take the city.

The fire did.

It never went out. That wasn't the plan — or maybe it was. The rituals are still running. The priests are still praying. The things the fire created to sustain itself are still here, still moving, still burning. They don't know the war ended. They don't know anything ended.

Some of the original inhabitants survived the initial burning. They burned anyway, eventually. Slowly. In pieces. What walks these halls is what they became.

The city's name is gone. What the thing from below was — gone too. You are descending into a victory that looks, from the inside, identical to a defeat.

The sweet smell is what's left of the people. The fire renders everything, given enough centuries.

## The Mechanic: BURN

### What It Is

Some enemies in the Ashen Crypts apply **BURN** on hit. Each application adds one burn stack. At the **start of your turn**, you take damage for each stack you carry. Stacks decay by 1 per turn if not refreshed.

Maximum stacks: **5**.

| Stacks | Damage per Turn |
|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Low |
| 2 | Moderate |
| 3 | Significant |
| 4 | Threatening |
| 5 | Lethal-adjacent |

### How Stacks Work

**Refreshing:** Any new burn application resets the decay timer on existing stacks — and adds a new one. Letting an enemy hit you twice doesn't stack linearly. It compounds.

**Decaying:** If no new burn is applied, you lose one stack at the end of your turn. At 3 stacks with no incoming hits, you'll clear in 3 turns — but that's 3 turns of taking damage while you try to find space.

**Maximum:** At 5 stacks, you are on fire in any meaningful sense. Dealing with this is the only priority.

### The Aggression Trap

This mechanic punishes passivity. If you hold back, circle, wait for openings — the stacks accumulate. You're burning while you hesitate. An aggressive engagement that ends in two turns does less total burn damage than a cautious one that drags into five.

Strike first. Strike often. End it.

The fire agrees with that philosophy. So did the city.

### Counters and Mitigation

**Ember Flask:** Clears all your burn stacks. One use. Rare.

**Ash Veil (artifact):** Reduces burn damage per stack. Doesn't eliminate it — just makes 3 stacks survivable rather than critical.

**Defeating the enemy quickly:** The only reliable counter. No enemy applying burn, no new stacks. Simple. Requires aggression.

**Void Ember (artifact):** Adds burn stacks to enemies on your strikes. The Crypts' logic, turned against its inhabitants.

### Example Turn Sequence

> You enter a room. A Flame Weaver is here. It strikes — 1 burn stack. Your turn begins: 1 damage. You strike back. It strikes again — 2 stacks. Your turn: 2 damage. You've taken 3 damage and accomplished little. The Weaver has 3 health remaining. Strike again. End it. The burn clears when the Weaver does.

### Strategic Notes for Content Generation

- High burn-stack situations should feel *urgent*, not analytical. Don't describe the player counting stacks. Describe heat, the smell of their own hair.
- Enemies that apply burn should move fast, attack often. They don't circle. They commit.
- Burn damage at turn start — describe it physically. A reminder that the body is already on fire.

## Bestiary

---

### Tier 1 — Common Horrors

---

#### Ember Husks

Charred corpses still moving. The fire kept them animate long after it finished killing them. They trail smoke from gaps in their skin. They are not fast. They don't need to be — every step leaves hot ash on the ground, and they swarm.

When killed, they collapse into a cloud of burning ash. Stand too close, and the cloud settles on you. Stand in it long enough, and the cloud reminds you that fire is patient even when the Crypts aren't.

**Behavior:** SHAMBLING (slow advance, relentless), COLLAPSING (ash cloud on death), TRAILING (hot ash underfoot)

*It walks. One foot, then the other. Smoke rises from its shoulders. It doesn't know it's dead. It doesn't know anything anymore.*

---

#### Cinder Priests

They were the architects of the city's final choice. They designed the rituals. They lit the first fires. They are still here, still praying, still feeding flames that have been burning for centuries. They haven't noticed the war ended. The prayers require full attention.

Their chants do something to the air around them. Enemies near a Cinder Priest hit harder, burn hotter. Killing the Priest first is not a tactic. It's survival literacy.

**Behavior:** CHANTING (empowering nearby enemies), IGNITING (applying burn at range), RETREATING (backing toward flames when threatened)

*It kneels before a fire that needs no more fuel. Its lips move. You can't hear the words. You can feel them.*

---

#### The Scorched

Warriors who were mid-charge when the fire took them. They didn't stop. Hundreds of years of forward motion, still burning, still attacking. The war logic burned into them before the flesh did. They exist as pure aggression now — no defense, no retreat, no hesitation.

They apply burn on contact. They are fast. They do not stop.

**Behavior:** CHARGING (straight-line, full commitment), BURNING (burn stacks on all contact), RELENTLESS (no defensive behavior, ever)

*It was running when it died. It is running now. It will still be running when you kill it.*

---

#### Flame Weavers

They shaped fire before the end. Bent it into forms. Made it listen. The fire still listens. Their craft survived the centuries better than their bodies did — they look like suggestions of people, heat-blur and orange light and hands that move with precise, terrible purpose.

They don't get close. They don't need to. Fire travels.

**Behavior:** WEAVING (ranged burn stacks, multiple hits), SHAPING (altering fire patterns — area denial), COMMANDING (redirecting other enemies into you)

*Its hands move like it's sewing. The fire follows the thread. You are where the needle points.*

---

#### Ashen Congregation

The city's civilian dead, fused by centuries of heat into something collective. Individual shapes press against the surface — a hand here, a face there, all of them merged into a shambling mass of charred bone and compacted ash. They were separate people once. The fire made them one.

They move slowly. They are large. When they strike, burn stacks come from more surface area than you'd prefer.

**Behavior:** ABSORBING (can incorporate nearby ash to grow), PRESSING (area attack, all-sides contact), MOURNING (the faces still move, still mouth words — distraction)

*You see faces in it. Dozens. They are all looking at you. They are all saying something. None of them agree.*

---

### Tier 1 — Shared Enemies Present in This Zone

---

#### Ash Children *(from primary bestiary)*

In the Crypts, they appear in larger numbers than anywhere else. They were here first. They are what the fire made of the city's youngest. They watch. The air near them is thin and overheated rather than cold.

---

#### Flickering Shades *(from primary bestiary)*

Here, they flicker between ash-cloud and form. The fire in this zone gives them more stability — they're harder to track, the teleports faster.

---

#### Bone Weavers *(from primary bestiary)*

The bones in these walls burned, but bone survives fire. The Weavers here emerge charred, arms blackened, grip no less strong.

---

### Tier 2 — Uncommon Threats

---

#### The Scorched *(also Tier 2 variant)*

See Tier 1 entry — but the Tier 2 variant is what the Scorched become when they've been burning long enough to accumulate mass. Larger. Faster. The burn application is on every attack, multiple stacks per hit. They have the same behavior, amplified.

*It leaves footprints in stone. It doesn't notice.*

---

#### Flame Weavers *(also Tier 2 variant)*

The senior practitioners. They survived the initial burning by design — the city's fire-shapers sealed themselves in living flame deliberately. Centuries have made their craft precise and vast. Area denial. Coordinated burns. They don't engage you directly. They build the room around you into a furnace.

**Behavior:** CONSTRUCTING (building fire-walls, sealing exits), COORDINATING (positioning other enemies), STUDYING (watching before acting)

*It watches you move. When it raises its hands, the room reorganizes around its intention.*

---

#### Ashen Congregation *(also Tier 2 variant)*

A Congregation that has absorbed enough ash — and enough bodies — to develop something like intelligence. It coordinates. It cuts off paths. The faces in its surface argue about what to do with you. Most of them vote the same way.

**Behavior:** HERDING (closing off exits), SPLITTING (can divide to flank), ABSORBING (will incorporate fallen enemies mid-fight)

*The mass stops. The faces confer. Then it splits. One half moves left. You didn't plan for that.*

---

### Creature Design Notes (Ashen Crypts)

1. **Everything here was human** — Unlike water-zone creatures, these were people. That should haunt every encounter.
2. **Fire means commitment** — No enemy in this zone hesitates. Write intent as action, not threat.
3. **Heat before sight** — You feel these creatures before you see them. Temperature, smell, sound of burning.
4. **Death leaves ash** — When they die, they leave something. Account for it.
5. **The burning is ongoing** — These creatures are mid-process. They didn't finish dying. They're still going.

## Boss: THE PYRE KEEPER

### Identity

A figure made of accumulated fire. Not flame-wreathed, not burning — made *of* it. The fire that's been burning here for centuries is also this. The fire is alive. It's been alive for a long time. It got lonely.

The Pyre Keeper is not a guardian. It didn't choose to stay. It didn't choose anything. The city's final ritual fused its consciousness with the collective fire, and the collective fire kept burning, and consciousness burns slowly. It has been burning through the same thoughts for centuries. The same needs.

It doesn't want to fight.

It wants company.

### Lore

There was a person here, once. A name that burned with the city's name. They designed the final ritual. They stood at its center. They believed in it.

The fire accepted them completely.

What remains remembers being human. It remembers warmth as something shared, not inflicted. It has been inflicting warmth for so long it can't remember the difference. When it approaches, it thinks it's offering comfort. It doesn't understand why everything it touches screams.

The spreading — when the fire gets lonely, it spreads — is a reflex. An old pattern. *If I can just make them warm enough, they'll stay.*

Everything that has stayed is ash.

### Fight Structure

**Phase 1 — The Greeting**

The Pyre Keeper moves toward you. Slowly. It has no idea this is a threat. Burn stacks accumulate just from proximity — passive aura. It tries to speak. The sound is a roar that forms words only if you listen very carefully.

> *"Stay. It is warm. I have been cold for—"*

It hasn't been cold. It doesn't know what it's saying.

**Intents:**
- APPROACHING (moving toward player, proximity burn building)
- REACHING (arm extended, inviting — single high burn application)
- SPREADING (fire expands to cover more floor — area denial)

**Phase 2 — The Desperation**

Below half health, it understands you're leaving. The fire responds to loneliness as it always has — expansion. The room begins to fill. Walls glow. The exits narrow.

> *"Please—"*

**Intents:**
- FLOODING (fire fills ground, forced movement)
- GRASPING (rapid multi-hit grab — high burn stack accumulation)
- CALLING (invokes nearby fire, empowers environment)
- COLLAPSING (pulls itself tighter and rushes — charge attack)

### Defeat

It doesn't extinguish. It diminishes. The fire contracts, concentrates, settles into a single ember that pulses on the floor of the now-dark chamber.

The room is still hot. The ash is everywhere. The ember continues to burn. You leave it burning.

You are not sure this was a victory.

*The Pyre Keeper makes a sound as it diminishes. It takes you a moment to identify it. You've heard it before, from people, in other contexts. It's the sound of something that expected something different.*

### Loot

The ember. You don't take it. But the knowledge it carries — how fire accumulates, how burn concentrates — crystallizes in you. Mechanically: burn stacks deal 25% more damage for the rest of the run. The fire gave you something on the way out. It always does.

### Why It Exists

The boss of the Ashen Crypts should make you feel guilty about winning. It is the most dangerous thing in the zone and also the saddest. Players who want to read the fight as combat can. Players who want to read it as tragedy can. Both readings are true.

*The fire spreads when it's lonely. Kill it and it's never lonely again. Live with that.*

## Items

### Zone-Specific

---

#### Void Ember
> *Taken from the cooling edge of a fire that has burned since before your grandfather's grandfather. It still thinks it's destroying something.*
**Effect:** Fire damage increased. Your strikes apply 1 burn stack. Aggression becomes self-sustaining.

---

#### Ash Veil
> *Woven from consolidated ash. It smells like a city. It smells like a choice.*
**Effect:** Burn damage per stack reduced. At 5 stacks, you no longer take damage from the first 2. You are still burning. You simply matter less about it.

---

#### Scorched Relic
> *An object from before. What object, impossible to say — the heat rendered specificity. It held meaning once. Now it holds heat.*
**Effect:** Passive: all your attacks carry a residual burn. One stack per hit, no additional cost. The fire follows your lead.

---

#### Ember Flask
> *The liquid inside is not water. It is what water becomes after centuries near the fire. It extinguishes. It also burns going down.*
**Effect:** Immediately clears all burn stacks. Single use. Consume when the stacks reach critical. The taste lingers.

## NPCs

---

### The Last Survivor

Someone who didn't burn. They were in the city when it happened. They have been in the city since. The fire didn't take them, and they have spent centuries trying to understand why.

They are not grateful for the exception. They watched everyone they knew burn. They have watched thousands of travelers descend and burn. They are tired in a way that centuries don't touch — tired the way only outliving everything can make a person.

They know the Crypts completely. They know what the fire wants. They tell you if you ask. Their voice doesn't modulate. It's been the same tone for very long.

**Dialogue style:** Flat. Unhurried. Not performatively sad — just depleted. Facts offered without affect.

> "The fire didn't take me. I've examined that from every direction. I have no explanation that satisfies. Neither does the fire."

> "The Pyre Keeper was a colleague. We studied together. I don't go near it anymore."

> "You could ask me how to survive this place. I have data. I've watched enough people die to have useful information."

**Function:** Lore delivery. Tactical information about the BURN mechanic and boss. Can trade burn-resistance items at cost of time — each conversation costs a small burn stack (the fire doesn't like witnesses to its failure). The price is worth it.

---

### The Ash Reader

The ash falls constantly here. It always has. Ash from walls, from ceiling, from the ambient burning of a city that never stopped. The Ash Reader has been interpreting the patterns for longer than is believable.

They are correct, frequently. Not always. The ash is not a precise instrument. But the futures they describe — your death, your path, the thing waiting in the next room — are specific enough to be unsettling. Specific enough to be useful.

They do not make eye contact. They are looking at the ash falling past your shoulder.

**Dialogue style:** Distracted. Mid-sentence pivots. Addresses the ash as often as you. What they say sounds like inference; what they see sounds like certainty.

> "The ash from the east passage is falling at a seven-degree angle. You've come from the water zone. The descent changed you. The fire reads that as — here, look at this cluster — it reads that as proximity. The fire finds you familiar."

> "That formation. Someone died near you recently. Not you. Someone carrying guilt. It's in the particulate."

> "You want to know about the boss. The ash says you'll survive the first phase. The second — that cluster there — unclear. I'd recommend moving east."

**Function:** Foreshadowing and wayfinding. Can reveal room types in sequence (the "map" equivalent for this zone). Reads burn stacks and comments on them — useful diagnostic, unsettling delivery.

---

### The Furnace Keeper

The ritual fires require maintenance. They always have. Someone had to tend them before the burning, and the burning didn't end that job — it made the job permanent. The Furnace Keeper has been maintaining the fires since the city ended itself. They don't consider this a tragedy. This is a vocation.

They trade. Their inventory is burn-adjacent: fuel, resistance, control. They speak about fire the way a craftsperson speaks about their material — neutrally, precisely, with great respect for its properties. They burned once. They got better. It informed their expertise.

**Dialogue style:** Professional. Precise. Proud of their work. No sentimentality about what the work costs. They are the one person in the Crypts who finds this place functional rather than horrific.

> "The northwest fire is underperforming. I'll correct that after this. What do you need?"

> "Burn resistance is a matter of exposure regulation, not avoidance. I can help with that. The price is fair."

> "The Pyre Keeper is not a problem. It is the fire at scale. I tend smaller versions of the same thing every day. The principle is identical. The difference is quantity."

**Function:** Item trading. Sells Ember Flasks, Ash Veils, and burn-related consumables at above-market rates for currency, or at fair rates for items taken from the Crypts' dead. Can upgrade the Scorched Relic for a price. Knows the Pyre Keeper's weaknesses — won't share them freely, but will trade.

## Audio Direction

### Philosophy

The Ashen Crypts are louder than the Threshold. The water zone is defined by dripping silence and dread through stillness. The fire zone is defined by constant ambient sound that never stops — because the fire never stops. The silence here is the wrong kind of silence. When something goes quiet, something is about to happen.

**Sound = urgency.** The crackle and pop of persistent fire keeps the player's nervous system engaged. Silence is threat, not comfort.

---

### Ambient Layers

**Base layer (constant):**
- Low, distant fire crackle — not roaring, just persistent presence
- Falling ash — a high, soft sound, almost like slow rain, irregular
- Deep stone heat-groan — thermal expansion, the buildings adjusting
- Ember pop — occasional, random, like knuckles cracking

**Mid layer (occasional):**
- Wind through burnt stone — there is no wind source. It doesn't matter.
- Structural settling — heavier than the baseline, implying instability
- Ash shifting — something moved through recently, or something is moving

**Unsettling layer (rare, triggered):**
- Chanting at the edge of hearing — the Cinder Priests, far off
- The sound of breathing that is also the sound of combustion
- A sound like a human voice that is actually fire moving through a specific gap in the stone

---

### Music

**Style:** Dark ambient / thermal drone

- **Instruments:** Bowed metal, low brass (distant), processed fire recordings, crumbling percussion
- **Distinguishing quality from Threshold:** More rhythmic than the water zone — not a beat, but a pulse. The fire has momentum.
- **Key:** Minor, but warmer frequencies than the Threshold. The horror here is not cold.

**Layers by game state:**

| State | Music Feel |
|-------|-----------|
| Exploring | Slow thermal pulse, occasional crackle texture, near-music |
| Approaching combat | Pulse accelerates slightly, dissonance enters on burns |
| In combat | Rhythmic urgency — not a beat, but building pressure |
| Victory | Pulse slows, crackle settles, brief thermal resolution |
| Death | Fire continues. Only your part of the sound stops. |
| Finding corpse | Crackle softens. Single sustained warm tone. Ash-fall prominent. |
| Boss approach | Everything builds, slow and inevitable, toward the Pyre Keeper's room |

---

### Sound Effects

**Movement:**
- Footsteps on ash — soft, dry, muffled. Weight pressing ash flat.
- Footsteps on charred stone — harder, echo absorbed by heat
- Moving through heavy ash — resistance, like shallow dry sand

**Combat:**
- Burn application — a sharp hiss, intimate. Like a hot coal on skin.
- Burn stacks ticking — a dry internal pulse. Body-first, not external.
- Player damage — sharp intake, effort. No gore sounds.
- Ember Husk collapse — a soft implosion, then settling ash.
- Flame Weaver attacks — the sound of fire moving with purpose. Not random crackle — *aimed* crackle.

**Environment:**
- Ritual fires activating — a resonant hum joining the ambient crackle
- Sealed door opening — stone grinding, pressure release, cooler air hiss
- Cache discovery — small warm sound, not chime — more like coals settling comfortably

**Creature Audio:**

**Ember Husks:**
- Movement: Dry shuffle, soft creak of charred joints, trailing crackle
- Idle: The pop of cooling embers, continuous
- Attack: Dry impact, then hiss of burn application
- Death: Soft implosion, ash settling, then silence in that spot

**Cinder Priests:**
- Movement: Robes brushing ash, deliberate, unhurried
- Idle: Chanting — low, rhythmic, in the city's lost language. Unsettling because it sounds almost like something you know.
- Attack: Chant peaks abruptly — a harsh, cracking syllable. Burn stacks spike.
- Death: Mid-word silence. The fire stutters in the room.

**The Scorched:**
- Movement: Rapid. The sound of something that is still charging.
- Idle: Constant combustion — they don't have idle states, but if they did, it would sound like this.
- Attack: Impact with heat — thud plus hiss, simultaneous.
- Death: The charge continues a half-second after death, then cuts. Silence.

**The Pyre Keeper:**
- Presence: The room's ambient fire doubles in volume. Warmth becomes oppressive.
- Movement: The fire doesn't have footsteps — the fire has *flow*. A directional sound.
- Speech: Fire shaped into approximation of voice. Words if you listen. Roar if you don't.
- Phase 2: Everything louder. The fire is desperate now.
- Defeat: A long, slow diminishment. The fire quieting, track by track, until only the ember remains.

---

### Room-Specific Audio

**EXPLORE:** Full ambient. Fire constant. Music near-absent. Ash-fall prominent.

**COMBAT:** Ambient ducks under creature sounds. Fire sounds become urgent, not comforting. Pulse music enters.

**CORPSE:** Ash-fall rises. Everything else softens. A single warm tone, mournful. Held while reading final words.

**CACHE:** Fire ambient softens to background. A brief, relative quiet — not safe, but less dangerous. Thermal drone resolves.

**EXIT:** Fire sounds recede behind you. The crackle fades with distance. Ahead: quiet, cool air sounds. What passes for silence after this zone.

---

### Technical Notes

- **Ash-fall layer:** Should loop seamlessly with randomized timing variations. Never rhythmic.
- **Fire crackle:** Multiple layers at different volumes and frequencies. Total = convincing. Individual = extractable for specific creature sounds.
- **Burn SFX:** Should feel bodily. The player character feels this. Not ambient — personal.
- **Silence as shock:** After the constant fire ambient, any moment of silence is immediately alarming. Use deliberately.

## Sample Room Sequence

A nine-room sequence illustrating zone pacing, mechanic introduction, and escalation.

---

**1. EXPLORE — The Descent**

> The stairs stop. Below, orange light. Not the flickering of torchlight — the steady, ancient glow of something that has been burning since before you were born. Heat rises to meet you before you take the first step down.

*Introduces the zone's sensory identity. Heat-first. Establishes that this burning is old.*

**2. EXPLORE — The Ashfall**

> Ash falls. Slow and continuous, from somewhere above that isn't sky. The floor is deep with it — your footprints are the first marks in what might be years. The passage ahead glows orange. The one behind you is already covering your tracks.

*Establishes the ash mechanic. Isolation in fire. Sense that time works differently here.*

**3. COMBAT — First Contact (Ember Husks)**

> Three shapes ahead, slow, trailing smoke. They tracked you by heat — you're cooler than anything else in this place. They move with the patience of things that have nowhere else to be. They are not fast. They will reach you eventually.

*First burn application. Teaches the mechanic gently: low stacks, single-husk collapse cloud to dodge.*

**4. EXPLORE — The Ritual Space**

> A fire burns in the center of the room with no fuel visible, no smoke, no explanation. The symbols on the floor predate whatever language you know. They mean something. The fire means something. A Cinder Priest knelt here recently — the ash around the fire shows the impression of knees. The prayer is ongoing, elsewhere.

*Builds lore. Foreshadows the Priest encounter. Player understands the fire is maintained, not natural.*

**5. COMBAT — Cinder Priest**

> It faces the ritual fire, not you. Doesn't turn when you enter. The nearby Husks — two of them — do. The Priest's prayer grows louder. The Husks hit harder while it prays.

*Teaches priority targeting. Demonstrates the Priest's empowerment mechanic. Tests aggression vs. caution.*

**6. CORPSE — The Hesitator**

> The ash is disturbed here — a wide area, the pattern of someone moving back and forth. They stopped here for a long time. The burn stacks that killed them are ash now too. Their message is carved carefully into the blackened wall: [FINAL MESSAGE].

*Emotional grounding. Shows what hesitation costs. Reinforces the zone's core lesson.*

**7. CACHE — The Sealed Alcove**

> A door that held. Inside: the first cool air since descending. Ash only at the threshold. Supplies left by someone who found this and kept it until they didn't need it anymore. The door closes behind you. For a moment — only a moment — the fire sounds distant.

*Rest beat. Brief safety. Ember Flask likely found here. Prepares player for final escalation.*

**8. COMBAT — Flame Weaver**

> It's already working when you enter. The floor near the far wall glows. One exit is closing — fire spreading in a deliberate line, not burning anything, just occupying space. The Weaver's hands move without pause. It knows you're there. It's accounting for you in its plan.

*High-pressure encounter. Tests aggressive resolution. High burn stack danger if player is passive.*

---

**9. EXIT — The Cooling**

> The temperature drops. Ash thins underfoot. The crackle behind you recedes like a tide. Stairs ahead, and air coming down them that tastes like nothing burned through it. The fire watches you leave. It doesn't move to stop you. It knows these passages. It knows where they lead. It will be here when someone else comes down.

*Exit. Victory with unease. The fire is patient even when the Crypts aren't.*

## Content Generation Guidelines

When generating room descriptions, combat narration, burn text, or death text for the Ashen Crypts:

1. **Fire is impatient. Writing should be too.** Shorter sentences than the Threshold. Urgency in structure, not just content.
2. **Second person, present tense. No exceptions.**
3. **Keep room descriptions under 50 words. Often under 35.**
4. **Lead with heat, smell, or sound — not sight.**
5. **Burn damage text should feel physical.** Not a status effect — a body experience. Heat in the chest. Skin tight. The smell of it.
6. **Never: exclamation marks, "suddenly," "very," "really," fourth wall breaks.**
7. **These were people.** Every enemy in this zone was a person who made a choice or had a choice made for them. The horror is human origin.
8. **Ash accumulates.** Reference it actively. The ash is everywhere. It's what's left.
9. **The fire never stops.** Ambient fire references in every room. It's the zone's heartbeat.
10. **Aggression is correct.** Combat narration should validate aggressive play. Hesitation described as costly, not cautious.
11. **Vary stack descriptions.** At 1 stack: mild warmth, distraction. At 3: heat becoming urgent. At 5: the word "burning" is appropriate and accurate.
12. **The Pyre Keeper is sad.** Any references to it — in lore, NPC dialogue, room flavor — should carry ambivalence, not menace.

### Fire Vocabulary Bank

**Use freely:**
- Char, ash, ember, cinder, pyre, soot, smolder
- Burn, ignite, consume, radiate, persist
- Dry, hot, suffocating, ancient, relentless
- Crumble, collapse, settle, drift (ash, not water)
- Orange, black, bone-white, dull red

**Avoid:**
- Inferno, blaze, conflagration (too large, too epic — this fire is intimate)
- Explosive, erupt, detonate (wrong energy — this fire is patient even when impatient)
- Warm (too comfortable — use "hot," "heat," "heat-blurred")
- Cozy, comforting, inviting (the fire thinks it's these things. You don't.)
