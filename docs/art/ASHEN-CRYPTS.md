# ASHEN CRYPTS — Zone Art Direction

## Zone Identity
A city that chose fire over surrender. The burning is deliberate, ritual, permanent. Centuries of ash have accumulated. Nothing here is extinguished — the fire continues by design.

## Palette
Deep orange-black, charcoal, bone-white ash, ember red. No amber — amber belongs to the Sunken Crypt. The light here is ORANGE-RED, not golden.

## Lighting
Multiple light sources from below — embers in cracks, firelight from unseen flames beneath the floor. Top of frame is dark. Bottom of frame glows orange-red. Ash particles float in the air, catching the upward light. No single torch — the fire is everywhere, distributed, rising.

## Style Modifiers
Fire and ash. Skin is cracked along the stress lines and ember-red glows from within — like coal with fire inside. Ash falls like snow, constant. Soot covers every surface in deep layers. Creatures are charred, burning, or made of accumulated fire. The horror here is the deliberate nature of the burning: this was chosen. The fire is not an accident. It is a ritual still in progress.

## Portrait Specs
Waist-up or full-body centered, vertical portrait 1024x1024. Subject fills the frame. Background is zone atmosphere only — charcoal dark with upward orange-red glow from below, ash particles in the air, no environmental detail.

### Ember Husks

```
Portrait of the Ember Husks — a creature card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite.
Orange-red firelight from below — the upward glow of embers in cracks in the floor illuminates the underside of the figure, leaving the top of the frame in deep charcoal shadow. Ash particles drift between the viewer and the subject.
A charred human corpse still moving — bones visible through gaps where the skin has burned away and the char has cracked open, revealing orange-red ember glow at the interior. The skin that remains is blackened and tight, splitting at the joints where movement stresses it. The head tilted forward, the face a mask of cracked char with two deep recesses where the eyes were — and in those recesses, the faintest ember-orange glow. Smoke trails from the shoulders, from the cracks in the skin, a continuous slow exhale. The posture is a step caught mid-stride — relentless, unhurried, still coming.
The horror detail: it is not burning. It burned completely, decades ago. The ember glow is what remains — the residual heat of something that consumed itself and kept going anyway.
Waist-up portrait, subject centered, no background — only charcoal dark above, orange-red ember glow from below, ash particles in the air.

Style: hyper-detailed dark fantasy digital painting, photorealistic, cinematic.
Orange-red upward firelight from below, charcoal dark above. Ash particles in mid-air.
Color palette: deep orange-black, charcoal, ember red at skin cracks, bone-white ash, smoke grey.
Cracked charred skin, interior ember glow, trailing smoke, ash particles. No bright colors, no neon, no cartoon style, no gradients.
Portrait orientation, creature centered, minimal background. Ultra detailed. 1024x1024.

### Flame Weavers

```
Portrait of the Flame Weavers — a creature card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite.
Orange-red firelight from below — but in this case the figure itself contributes light. A heat-blur distortion surrounds the subject. Charcoal dark above. Ash particles glow briefly as they pass through the warmer air near the figure.
Not quite a body — the suggestion of a person assembled from heat-blur and orange light. The outline is roughly human: two arms, a torso, a head. But the interior is fire shaped like flesh — the hands are the most defined, the most present, the most real. The hands move with precision and terrible purpose, tracing patterns in the air that briefly hold their shape as fire-geometry before dissipating. The face, if it has one, is deep within a column of rising hot air visible only as distortion. It does not need to approach. The fire it shapes crosses the distance without it.
The horror detail: the fire-geometry the hands trace in the air looks like words. You do not know the language. You know they are addressed to you.
Waist-up portrait, subject centered, no background — only charcoal dark above, orange-red from below, heat-blur distortion around the figure, glowing ash particles.

### The Pyre Keeper (Boss)

```
Portrait of The Pyre Keeper — boss creature card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite.
Orange-red firelight from everywhere — the subject IS fire. The light radiates outward from the figure rather than falling on it. Charcoal dark at the very top of frame, but the middle and bottom are warm from the boss itself. Ash falls into the light and is consumed.
A figure assembled from accumulated fire — roughly human-shaped but made entirely of flame given density. The core is deep orange-red, almost liquid, moving in the slow deliberate circulation of something that has been burning through the same thoughts for centuries. The outer surface: fire cooled slightly at the edges into something with texture — the suggestion of a torso, a face, hands. The face, if it can be called that: two deeper chambers of fire where eyes would be, the impression of features below them, the shape of a mouth. The posture is not aggressive — it is the posture of something waiting for company. Arms open, slightly. Weight distributed as if it has been standing here waiting for a very long time.
The horror detail: the fire in the boss's chest is patterned. If you watch the movement of the flame, it repeats — the same slow circulation, the same thoughts, cycling for centuries. The pattern contains something that was once a person.
Full-body portrait, figure centered, no background — the figure itself illuminates the frame, charcoal dark only at the edges and top.

Style: hyper-detailed dark fantasy digital painting, photorealistic, cinematic. Boss portrait — fills the frame with presence, fire is the primary substance.
Light radiates outward from the subject. Charcoal dark at frame edges.
Color palette: ember red and deep orange dominant for the figure, charcoal dark at extreme frame edges, bone-white ash consumed in the figure's light, smoke grey rising.
Fire-substance figure, internal circulation of flame visible, face implied in the ember pattern, ash particles burning on contact. No bright colors, no neon, no cartoon style, no gradients.
Portrait orientation, boss centered and filling the frame, minimal background. Ultra detailed. 1024x1024.
```
