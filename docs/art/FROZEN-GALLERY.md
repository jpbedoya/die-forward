# FROZEN GALLERY — Zone Art Direction

## Zone Identity
Something exhaled once, deep beneath the named places, and everything stopped. The Gallery preserves its dead perfectly. Time does not pass here — it accumulates.

## Palette
Pale blue, crystal white, grey stone, faint warm amber TRAPPED inside ice. The amber is NOT the lighting — it is imprisoned in the ice walls, visible within them, emanating from inside rather than falling from outside.

## Lighting
Diffuse and cold — no single source. Light is trapped inside the ice walls, emanating dimly from all directions equally. Cold blue-white overall. Occasional amber warmth visible deep inside ice formations — like a torch frozen mid-flame, visible through translucent ice but not providing warmth. The light is ancient and imprisoned. Nothing here casts a strong directional shadow.

## Style Modifiers
Ice and perfect preservation. Creatures are encased in, made of, or emerging from ice. Ice surfaces are translucent — things visible inside them. Breath clouds from any warm subject visible in cold air. Frost on every surface, layered, geological. The horror here is perfect preservation — these things are intact, undecayed, exactly as they were at the moment of freezing. They just resumed moving.

## Portrait Specs
Waist-up or full-body centered, vertical portrait 1024x1024. Subject fills the frame. Background is zone atmosphere only — cold blue-white diffuse light, ice surfaces visible with amber trapped within, breath clouds, frost. No environmental detail.

### The Preserved

```
Portrait of The Preserved — a creature card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite.
Cold diffuse blue-white light from all directions equally — no single source. The light comes from inside the ice walls, trapped there for decades. Faint amber visible deep within the ice behind the subject, imprisoned warmth that provides no warmth. Breath cloud from the creature's mouth visible — it is thawing toward the warmth of the viewer.
A former pilgrim in the garments of their descent — intact, not rotted, not damaged — the cold preserved everything exactly as it was. The clothing is correct, the boots still good, a pack still on one shoulder. The face shows the last expression clearly: not fear, not pain — the expression of someone interrupted. Cheeks slightly flushed with decades of sustained cold. Eyes open. Both arms extended forward and slightly down, reaching toward the nearest warmth with the patience of something that has been reaching for decades and has only now gained enough motion to make progress. Ice crystals along the eyebrows and collar.
The horror detail: there is still color in the lips. It shouldn't be there. The cold preserved it. It looks like the person might be about to speak.
Waist-up portrait, subject centered, no background — only cold blue-white diffuse light, faint amber trapped in ice behind, breath cloud visible at the mouth.

Style: hyper-detailed dark fantasy digital painting, photorealistic, cinematic.
Cold diffuse blue-white light, no directional source. Amber warmth visible only inside ice formations in the background.
Color palette: pale blue dominant, crystal white, grey stone, faint amber trapped in ice, frost white on fabric and hair.
Intact preserved clothing, ice crystals on surfaces, visible breath cloud, diffuse cold light. No bright colors, no neon, no cartoon style, no gradients.
Portrait orientation, creature centered, minimal background. Ultra detailed. 1024x1024.
```

```
Portrait of the Frost Sentinels — a creature card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite.
Cold diffuse blue-white light equally from all directions. The Sentinel's armor catches the diffuse light on every flat surface — a uniform cold sheen. Faint amber visible deep within the ice of the background walls, imprisoned light.
A figure in ancient armor — massive, broader than a person, standing with the patient stillness of something that has not moved in decades and has only now begun to. The armor is extraordinary: layered, archaic, every plate precise and intact, sealed with accumulated ice that has filled the joints and grown between the plates into translucent growths. The shoulder accumulation is particularly thick — years of frost forming against stationary armor, geometric and layered like sediment. The visor reflects the diffuse cold light back at the viewer. One arm is raising — slowly, with the grinding effort of ice-packed joints and geological patience. The beginning of the movement, not the completion.
The horror detail: the ice in the shoulder joints is cracking. It has been cracking for years. The Sentinel has been attempting this motion for a very long time. Now, finally, the ice is giving way.
Full-body portrait, subject centered, no background — only cold blue-white diffuse light, ice sediment on armor surfaces, faint amber trapped in background ice.
```

### The Shattered

```
Portrait of The Shattered — a creature card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite.
Cold diffuse blue-white light from all directions. The fragments of the subject each catch the diffuse light independently — translucent ice-piece surfaces, each one a small lens. Breath clouds from the main mass, where residual warmth remains.
A figure that froze and cracked rather than holding intact — now existing as multiple large pieces, each one a fragment of what was once a whole person, each fragment moving independently and with independent intention. The largest piece: a torso and partial head, the face visible on the cracked surface, expression frozen in the moment of fracture. The arms: separate, each one its own entity, moving.
```
