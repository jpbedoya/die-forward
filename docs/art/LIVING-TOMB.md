# LIVING TOMB — Zone Art Direction

## Zone Identity
The tomb does not have a beginning anyone can trace. It grew while no one was watching. Every body here became part of what keeps it warm. The horror is that it looks alive and warm — not dead and cold.

## Palette
Deep wet red, sickly green, grey-pink membrane, pale yellow-white where things have died inside. No clean light.

## Lighting
NO torch. Bioluminescent — dim pulsing light from the organic material itself. Green-tinged, sickly, from below and within surfaces. Light emanates from the living walls, the floor membrane, from veins running through the growth. Everything is slightly wet and catches this biological glow. The light pulses with the tomb's heartbeat — slow, deep, regular.

## Style Modifiers
Living organic surfaces. Creatures here emerge FROM the walls/floor or are partially absorbed into them. Everything is wet — membrane sheen, viscous fluid, moisture on every surface. Veins visible through semi-translucent tissue. The warmth is the horror: body temperature, everywhere, a warmth that should not exist underground. The wrongness is not cold and dead — it is warm and alive and that is worse.

## Portrait Specs
Waist-up or full-body centered, vertical portrait 1024x1024. Subject fills the frame. Background is zone atmosphere only — living organic surfaces, bioluminescent green glow from within the material, no torch, no clean light. Wet everything.

### Mycelium Crawlers

```
Portrait of the Mycelium Crawlers — a creature card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite.
Bioluminescent green light from below and within the organic floor — sickly, pulsing at the rhythm of the tomb's heartbeat. No torch. The underside of the creature catches the green glow; the top is in relative shadow. Everything is wet.
A creature that was never human — low to the floor, fast-movement posture, body covered in a membrane that catches the bioluminescent glow. The leg count is wrong — there are too many, and the count shifts when you look directly at the attachment points. Each leg ends in a fine point, designed for surface adhesion. The body is segmented, the segments slightly translucent — organic material visible within. The head, if it is a head, faces forward: two clusters of pale eyes, no pupils, set into the membrane. The thorax behind the head is distended — full of spores, visibly full, the membrane stretched in a way that suggests what would happen on impact. One leg is slightly raised — mid-step, caught between bursts of motion.
The horror detail: there are three more in the background, visible only as bioluminescent eye clusters reflecting green light in the dark. They are equidistant. They have been following at the same distance since you entered the room.
Full-body portrait, creature centered and close, no background — only bioluminescent green organic floor material and the eye-cluster reflections in the dark behind.

Style: hyper-detailed dark fantasy digital painting, photorealistic, cinematic.
Bioluminescent green light from the organic floor — pulsing, sickly, no torch. Wet membrane surfaces catching the green glow.
Color palette: deep wet red and grey-pink membrane, sickly green bioluminescent light, pale yellow-white of the spore-swollen thorax, dark wet surfaces overall.
Translucent segment membranes, too many legs, spore-filled thorax, wet everything, green bioluminescent underlight. No bright colors, no neon, no cartoon style, no gradients.
Portrait orientation, creature centered, minimal background. Ultra detailed. 1024x1024.
```

### The Root (Boss) — Environmental Portrait

```
Portrait of The Root's chamber — boss location card for Die Forward, a dark roguelite. The Root is not a creature with a body. It IS the room. This is an environmental portrait: the final chamber as the monster.
Bioluminescent green light from every surface — strongest here, in the oldest part of the tomb, brighter and more established than anywhere above. The light pulses in a rhythm that has become uncomfortably synchronized with the viewer's own heartbeat. No torch. No ceiling — what is above is too close and too organic to be called a ceiling. Everything wet.
A chamber that is not a chamber anymore. The walls are the same material as the creature — there is no distinction between room and Root. The floor rises in places — not stone structures, but upward-growing tissue, deliberate, aimed. The ceiling has come down in others — long trailing root-structures hanging close to the head height. From the left wall: a wall-slab that has tilted forward, a reach. From the right wall: fingers. Not hand-shaped — Root-shaped — long taproot structures ending in fine tendrils, extended into the room's center space. From the floor: the floor itself has lifted in a ring, enclosing the space. The lighting pulses. The walls breathe. The room contracts very slightly between one frame and the next.
The horror detail: there is no exit visible from this angle. The passage behind is closed. The room is not large. It is becoming smaller. The root-structures extending from every surface are not attacking — they are measuring.
Full-frame environmental portrait — the chamber as the monster. The subject is the room itself. No single creature centered — the horror is spatial, every surface, omnidirectional.

Style: hyper-detailed dark fantasy digital painting, photorealistic, cinematic. Environmental boss portrait — wide spatial composition within the portrait frame.
Bioluminescent green from every surface, pulsing, synchronized, strongest and most established here.
Color palette: deep wet red and grey-pink membrane dominant, sickly green bioluminescence from every wall and floor, pale yellow-white of older incorporated material, organic dark in the compressed space.
Room-as-creature, wall-surfaces reaching, floor lifting, ceiling descending, root-tendrils from all surfaces, organic fluid everywhere, total enclosure. No bright colors, no neon, no cartoon style, no gradients.
Portrait orientation, chamber filling the entire frame, the room itself is the subject. Ultra detailed. 1024x1024.
```
